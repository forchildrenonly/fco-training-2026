Training 1 of 6

Positive & Supportive Caregiver Child Interactions

This training focuses on developmentally appropriate interactions for each age group — from infants to school-age children. Led by Crystal, a licensed clinical social worker, it covers TRS Category 2 requirements: responding warmly to children's cues, naming emotions, guiding behavior with positive language, supporting transitions, and co-regulating children's emotions. Key takeaways include age-specific strategies (narrating for infants, offering two simple choices for toddlers, open-ended questions for pre-K), why staying calm as a teacher is the single biggest factor in classroom management, and how TRS evaluates teacher responses — not child behavior.

Training 2 of 6

Play-Based Learning: Let's Make Learning Fun

Presented by Kelsey from Friendswood, this session explains why play is the most powerful way children learn. Play-based learning is child-initiated and teacher-guided, and supports brain development, language growth, social-emotional skills, and physical coordination. Types of play include free play, sensory play, dramatic play, and cooperative play. Practical ideas include sensory bags, laminated reusable activity sheets, learning centers, and weekly lesson plans. Key stat: children learn through play after 20 repetitions vs. 400 for textbook-style learning. Teachers are encouraged to use TikTok, ChatGPT, and Google for creative, themed activity ideas — and to document everything in ProCare!

Training 3 of 6

Effective Communication with Parents

Presented by Miss Dawn, this training covers how to build strong, trust-based relationships with parents. Core strategies include active listening, giving regular updates through ProCare, using the "sandwich method" (positive → concern → positive), being transparent and honest while remaining empathetic, and using "we/us" language to foster teamwork. For difficult conversations, always include a director or neutral third party. TRS places huge emphasis on individual, specific written communication with parents — not just group posts. Teachers should never share personal phone numbers, and should always have a director review sensitive messages before sending.

Training 4 of 6

Food Program Training (CACFP)

Miss Dawn walks through the Child and Adult Care Food Program (CACFP), which reimburses FCO for providing healthy meals to children who may not get nutritious food at home. All required food components must be served — children don't have to eat them, but they must be placed in front of each child. Lunch requires 5 components: vegetable, fruit, grain, meat/meat alternative, and milk. Snack requires 2 of 4. BLPs (meal count sheets) must be completed at the point of service — real time, black ink only, no corrections by teachers. Food allergy substitutions require a doctor's note. Two-percent milk is not acceptable — only whole milk (age 1), or 1%/fat-free (ages 2+).

Training 5 of 6

Supporting Children of All Abilities

Guest speaker Patricia, a licensed speech-language pathologist with 36+ years of experience, explains how to recognize developmental milestones and identify children who may need additional support. Speech impairment is closely tied to autism, and autism diagnoses have been rising dramatically since 2007. Teachers are given milestone checklists (from birth through school age) and encouraged to refer families to River Kids Therapy, which provides speech, occupational, and physical therapy directly at FCO locations. The company accepts Medicaid and most commercial insurance. OT can help with handwriting, low tone, sensory issues; PT with motor milestones like hopping, jumping, and skipping.

Training 6 of 6

Tech Training: ChildcareEd Continuing Education

Taylor and Crystal walk through ChildcareEd.com, the new online platform for state-required continuing education. Staff use the email address linked to their Paychex account to log in (first-time users click "Forgot Password"). To request a course: find it on ChildcareEd, copy the URL, and email it to Crystal and your director — they will assign credits to your account. Total requirement is 30 training hours per year (TRS standard), which includes instructor-led training days like this one plus state-required online courses. Staff can choose elective courses based on their age group or interests. CDA certification is also available through the platform.
